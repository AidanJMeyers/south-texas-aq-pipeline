Site Count Reconciliation

Reconciling the Count: 42 active + 5 non-scope = 47 Total An earlier version of this report listed 43 sites with active data, but that count included Calaveras Lake Park (AQSID 480291609), which was later confirmed to measure only Total Suspended Particulate (TSP) — a pollutant outside this project's scope (PM2.5, PM10, O3, CO, NOx, SO2, VOCs). After the v0.3.2 pipeline ingestion, the correct count is 42 active sites with in-scope data, plus 5 sites outside active analysis (3 CPS fence-line reference-only, 1 Calaveras Lake Park TSP-only excluded, 1 Williams Park disabled). The total inventory remains 47 unique monitoring sites.

Audit Methodology

This inventory was compiled on April 13, 2026 by auditing all data sources in the project pipeline:

Step 1: Processed CSV Scan Extracted unique (aqsid, site_name, county_name, data_source) tuples from all 7 By_Pollutant CSV files in 01_Data/Processed/By_Pollutant/. As of v0.3.2 (April 15, 2026), this identifies 42 unique AQS IDs with active in-scope pollutant data (up from 41 after the Corpus Christi Palm VOCs ingestion).
Step 2: TCEQ Reference Cross-Check Compared against Extra TCEQ Sites.xlsx in !Final Raw Data/, which lists all 18 TCEQ supplemental monitoring sites originally identified for the study area. Found 6 AQS IDs present in the reference but absent from the processed CSVs.
Step 3: Reconciliation Combined both sources into a unified 47-site registry. Identified network attribution (EPA, TCEQ, or dual), pollutant coverage per site, and flagged reference-only entries with no active data. County name capitalization inconsistencies (BEXAR vs. Bexar, NUECES vs. Nueces) were normalized for this report.
